# Creator Onboarding Engine Blueprint v1

## 1. Purpose

Creator Onboarding helps a hobby maker become a successful Creator Partner.

The engine should guide a maker from early idea or hobby activity into a clear partner profile, first product portfolio, readiness score, and first success missions. The goal is to make the first steps practical, encouraging, and structured.

## 2. Creator Profile

A Creator Profile should include:

- `creatorId`: Unique identifier for the creator.
- `displayName`: Public or internal creator name.
- `activities`: The creative activities the creator can offer.
- `brands`: One or more creator brands or activity identities.
- `productCount`: Number of products currently available or planned.
- `existingChannels`: Places where the creator already sells or promotes work.
- `supportNeeds`: Areas where the creator needs help.
- `readinessScore`: Simple score showing how ready the creator is to publish and receive orders.

## 3. Activity Portfolio

One creator can have multiple activities.

Each activity can have its own logo, brand identity, and 5-6 starter products. This lets a creator grow in more than one direction without forcing all work into one brand or one category.

Examples:

- Candles
- Wooden Gifts
- Embroidery
- 3D Printing
- Educational Products
- Repair Services

## 4. Onboarding Questions

The first onboarding flow should ask:

- What do you make?
- How many products do you have?
- Do you already sell somewhere?
- What help do you need?

These answers help the platform understand the creator's current stage and what support will be most useful.

## 5. Creator Readiness Score

Creator Readiness Score is a simple score that shows how ready a creator is to publish products and receive orders.

It can evaluate:

- Product photos
- Descriptions
- Pricing
- Packaging
- Shipping readiness
- Quality review

The score should be supportive, not punitive. It should help creators understand what to improve next.

## 6. First Success Missions

First Success Missions are small practical goals that help a creator build momentum.

Missions:

- Add first product
- Improve product photos
- Complete description
- Publish first offer
- Receive first order
- Receive first review

These missions turn onboarding into a guided path instead of a blank dashboard.

## 7. Future Engines

Future engines can expand creator support:

- Partner Success Engine
- Opportunity Engine
- Growth Engine
- Community Engine
